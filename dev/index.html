About me:

I am a second year PhD student in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science department at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, where I study control theory. I am advised by Necmiye Ozay and Stephane Lafortune. I graduated from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville in May 2018 with a B.S. in electrical engineering and double major in mathematics.

My research:

I am interested in the broad area of cyberphysical systems within the frameworks of hybrid systems and discrete event systems. I am currently working on abstraction- based control, controlled invariant set computation, and opacity verification and enforcement.
